Born in 1994 of Sisseton-Whapeton Sioux and Turtle Mountain Cree descent, Randy Plummer was raised by a single Native American parent who swiftly instilled the importance of family, tradition, and cultue at a young age. These aspects continue to motivate him today in his endeavor to achieve his Ethnomusicology and Teaching degrees!

After a surprise family trip to Disneyland for his 5th birthday, he and his family ran into the Dixyland band playing in New Orleans Square which completely mezmorized the young boy. After eyeing each member of the band, he would continually return to the Saxophone. Shortly after the band had played their rendition of "Happy Birthday," the soon-to-be musician swore that he would grow up and play the Saxophone one day!

Now a vetted multi-instrumentalist, capable composer and exceptional soloist, Randy Plummer continues to his develop his love for music and cultural preservation by learning his traditional language, including songs along with stories, and focus on composing new and unique musical material from a Native perspective! Currently Randy Plummer resides in Riverside and attends the Universidty of California, Riverside where he plans to graduate in the future with his Doctoriate focusing on Native American / Indigenous Studies.
